About

Welcome to my website. I'm Elija Perrier. I'm an interdisciplinary mathematical physicist and researcher specialising in quantum–classical AI systems integration, multi‑agent modelling and AI alignment using optimal control theory. A bit more about me:

  • I publish research on quantum machine learning, AI risk measurement and governance, and multi‑agent systems.
  • I completed my PhD in quantum machine learning at the University of Technology, Sydney and my other Bachelor degrees at the University of Sydney, NSW, and Murdoch University in Perth, Western Australia.
  • I have a multi-disciplinary background spanning physics, computer science, mathematics, economics, law and philosophy.
  • I've been a lawyer for over 20 years as a practitioner and legal academic across Australia, Europe and the UK. I currently work in investment banking.
  • I'm also a software engineer proficient in a range of frontend and backend languages (Python mostly), stack developer (AWS) and HPC/Singularity.

Current activities

I am currently an honorary fellow at the Centre for Quantum Software and Information at UTS, Sydney where I completed my PhD. In quantum, my focus is on classical and quantum AGI, especially quantum agents. I am also an a research affiliate with the Gradient Institute based out of the University of Sydney. There I work on mostly AI alignment and control/measurement. I was previously a visiting scholar at Cambridge Law School and a fellow at the Stanford Center for Responsible Quantum Technology.
